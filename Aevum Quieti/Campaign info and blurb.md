Sunday, February 18th, 1691
>I had a peculiar dream last night, and in my efforts to keep the last vestiges of the memory available to me, I have written below the events I remember.
>I am in a place of sharp angles, unnaturally twisted, hard to comprehend. Even trying to remember them makes me dizzy. Odd beings surround me, some shadowy and ethereal, some solid white and black, swirling like an ink drop, drop, dropped into water. I smell burning, cloth and flesh alike, pungent, making it hard to breathe. The figures swarm me, chanting in a language I can't understand, suddenly vanishing in puffs of smoke. Quiet envelopes the space, those unnatural corners swirling.
>This part has faded from memory, but the next part I remember, I am being tossed about by a violent sea of blood. I struggle to breathe, again, each breath marred by a red downpour, and waves that take the opportunity to splash down my throat, forcing their way into my lungs. Sheer will keeps me afloat and able to breathe. Something happens that escapes me, but I awake, trembling, with tears mingling with sweat. My pillow is soaked, and I can't tell whether my sweat or my tears that have soaked my pillow.
>Thomas is gone, a light flickering through the low door frame into the room, the shadows teasing me. I'm hot, so hot, I need to escape, go somewhere cool. I stand and quietly walk through the door and outside into the crisp night. I sat for a while. Eventually, gray streaks of light and birds that sing of the dawn and day to come.
>The days come and pass, and I feel as if I watch myself from the outside in. Thomas rarely consoles me, even though I know he is in as much as I am. I quietly pray for the day we heal, but it doesn't seem possible.
>-Sylvia
