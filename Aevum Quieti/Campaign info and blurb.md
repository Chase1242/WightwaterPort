### Rough draft
Sunday, February 19th, 1690
>We've been selected by the Church for being "upstanding citizens." I suspect the recent racket created by my husband Antonio in the town square was the cause. I'm looking forward to the voyage on the Vezetuu Martin. It should be a relief from the monotony of the childcare and house chores.
>I am unsure of when the voyage will take place, but the resources required take time to gather, especially by the Crown, which moves slower than most
>I had a peculiar dream last night, and in my efforts to keep the last vestiges of the memory available to me, I have written below the events I remember.
>I am in a place of sharp angles, unnaturally twisted, hard to comprehend. Even trying to remember them makes me dizzy. Odd beings surround me, some shadowy and ethereal, some solid white and black, swirling like ink mixed into water. I smell burning, cloth and flesh alike, pungent, making it hard to breathe. The figures swarm me, chanting in a language I can't understand, suddenly vanishing in puffs of smoke. Quiet envelopes the space, those unnatural corners swirling. 
>This part has faded from memory, but the next part I remember, I am being tossed about by a violent sea of blood. I struggle to breathe, again, each breathe marred by a red downpour, and waves that take the opportunity to splash down my throat, forcing it's way into my lungs. Sheer will keeps me afloat, and able to breathe. Something happens that escapes me, but I awake, trembling, with tears mingling with sweat. My pillow is soaked and I can't tell whether my sweat or tears soak my pillow.
>Thomas is gone, a light flickering through the low door frame into the room, the shadows teasing me. I'm hot, so hot, I need to escape, go somewhere cool. I stand, and quietly walk through the door and outside into the crisp night. I sat for awhile. Eventually, gray streaks of light and birds that sing of the dawn and day to come. 
>The days come and pass, I feel as if I watch myself from outside in. Thomas rarely consoles me, even though I know he is in as much as I am. I quietly pray for the day we heal, but it doesn't seem possible.
> -Sylvia
